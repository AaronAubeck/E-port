RMPP_PCOM7E Research Methods and Professional Practice

Within this Module there were 12 individual units, looking at ways of researching and developing skills. This was rounded up with 3 assignments. A literature review, a research presentation and a reflective piece.

Unit 1

  • Selected research topic
  • Initial Collaborative post
  • Reasoning Quiz
  • Reflective activity- Ethics in computing

The module began with unit 1 where we looked at ethics and research within the computer science field. This interested me as i am training to become a data compliance officer, eithics within data collecting is necessary to protect peoples infomation.

It was through this unit that i was able to understand how to navigate, planning and researching for one and ultimately build my knowledge and confidence to write my own.

Unit 3 allowed me to investigate research design, this is a curcial aspect of conducting research. It provides a systematic plan for collecting, analysing and interpreting data to answer research questions. I learnt that a well-designed research plan is necessary to ensure that research is valid, reliable and ethical.

Unit 4

  • Started research for literature review

This unit looked at observation methods, which are crucial for conducting primary research. They involve systematically observing and recording data to gain insights into behaviors, attitudes and experiences which will help me overall with my keystone project and further my abilities within my job. Throughout this unit i have taken note of the need to select an apropriate methodology, establish a protocol for collecting and recording data and remembering to address ethical considerations.

Unit 5

  • Interview and survey design lecturecast

Gathering and understanding information given through surveys and interviews is essential for research projects and business in general. One crucial point i have learnt in this unit is that researchers should choose the method that suits thier research question, sample size and data type, and take steps to minimize potential biases and ensure data accuracy.
